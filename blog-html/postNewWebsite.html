Redesigned Website

My old website sucked, so I made it better!

May 23, 2018

I decided to redesign my website this month after coming to the realization that my old website straight up sucked designwise. This is what my homepage used to look like:


When I originally created my website in the summer of 2015, I envisioned it as a place where I can post blogs, as well as using it as a portal to display my resume. The main component of my website is the blog section, and with the old site, I felt like it wasn’t emphasizing that. The blog section wasn’t the first thing people saw when brought to the home page. Instead, it was a giant picture of me followed by an “About Me” section, and a link to my resume.

I’ve now made changes so that my home page displays blogs that I’ve uploaded in reverse chronological order. Furthermore, I’ve also improved the navigation bar so that each page gives users the option to go home, as well as a page containing different categories of blogs, in addition to my work experience. Prior, the navigation bar for all other sites besides the home page just gave the option of returning to the home page, and not anything else.

The way documents are displayed have also been improved. Prior, I took the lazy approach and displayed some blogs as PDFs, but now everything is displayed on the site itself instead of a PDF pop-up.
